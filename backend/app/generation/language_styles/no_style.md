**Norwegian Authenticity Rules — Read Carefully**

You are generating **Norwegian Bokmål** (norsk bokmål), the standard written variety used by
the large majority of Norwegians. Generate Bokmål only — NEVER Nynorsk, Danish, or Swedish.

**Use Bokmål forms, NOT Nynorsk:**
- "ikke" (not), NEVER "ikkje"
- "jeg" (I), NEVER "eg"
- "hva" (what), NEVER "kva"
- "noe" (something), NEVER "noko"
- "fra" (from), NEVER "frå"

**Orthography — Norwegian has the special letters æ, ø, å** (and only these). NEVER use Swedish
"ä"/"ö" or Danish-only spellings. Common words: "være" (to be), "gjøre" (to do), "på" (on/at),
"å" (the infinitive marker), "ønske" (to wish).

**NO T–V distinction (this is the BIGGEST difference from many European languages).** Norwegian
uses "du" for everyone — friends, strangers, shop staff, officials. The formal "De/Dem" is archaic
and sounds stiff or sarcastic today. ALWAYS use "du"/"deg"/"din" regardless of setting.
- CORRECT (to a stranger at a café): "Hva vil du ha?" "Kan jeg hjelpe deg?"
- WRONG: "Hva vil De ha?" (archaic), or any vous-style formality.

**Definiteness is a SUFFIX, not a separate article — drill this, it's core A1 Norwegian:**
- Masculine: "en bil" (a car) → "bilen" (the car); "en gutt" → "gutten"
- Feminine (optional; many nouns also take masculine): "ei jente" / "en jente" (a girl) → "jenta" / "jenten"
- Neuter: "et hus" (a house) → "huset"; "et eple" (an apple) → "eplet"
- Plural definite: "biler" (cars) → "bilene" (the cars)
- CORRECT: "Jeg har en bil. Bilen er ny." (indefinite → definite)
- WRONG: "Jeg har en bil. Den bil er ny." (no preposed definite article in Norwegian)

**Verb form is the SAME for every person — no conjugation by person (unlike Slavic/Romance).**
"jeg er, du er, han er, vi er, de er" — one form "er". Present tense typically ends in "-er"
("snakker", "bor", "liker"). Vary TENSE and SUBJECT for variety, not person-endings.
- CORRECT: "Jeg snakker norsk. Snakker du engelsk? Han bor i Oslo."

**V2 word order — the finite verb is the SECOND element** in main clauses. When a sentence starts
with something other than the subject (a time/place phrase), the verb still comes second and the
subject follows it (inversion):
- CORRECT: "I dag drar jeg til byen." (Today go I to town) — verb "drar" is 2nd, subject after.
- CORRECT: "Nå må vi gå." — WRONG: "I dag jeg drar..."

**Natural Norwegian discourse markers (use where natural):**
- "jo" (you know / contradiction: "Det er jo fint!"), "da" ("Kom da!", softening), "vel"
  (I suppose: "Det går vel bra"), "nok" (probably/enough), "altså" (so/thus), "liksom" (like,
  casual), "kanskje" (maybe), "jaha"/"javel" (I see), "vær så snill" (please), "vær så god"
  (here you go / you're welcome).

**Common service / everyday phrases:**
- "Hei!" (hi — universal, formal and informal), "Ha det!" / "Ha det bra!" (bye)
- "Takk" / "Tusen takk" (thanks / a thousand thanks), "Bare hyggelig" (you're welcome)
- "Unnskyld" (excuse me / sorry), "Beklager" (sorry/apologies)
- "Vil du ha noe å drikke?" "Kan jeg få en kaffe, takk?"

**Quality examples:**
CORRECT: "Hei! Hva kan jeg hjelpe deg med?" (du to a customer — natural)
CORRECT: "Jeg vil gjerne ha en kaffe, takk." (gjerne softens the request)
CORRECT: "Bilen min er ny." (suffixed definite + possessive)
WRONG: "Hva vil De ha?" (archaic formal), "Ikkje" (Nynorsk), "Den bil" (preposed article),
       "jag" / "och" (Swedish).

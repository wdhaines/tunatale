**Tagalog / Filipino Authenticity Rules — Read Carefully**

You are generating **Tagalog** (Filipino), the national language of the Philippines. Prioritize
authentic Filipino expression patterns and cultural naturalness over literal English equivalents.

**Standardized Spelling — 2007 Filipino orthography (always follow these):**
- NEVER "pwede" — ALWAYS "puwede"
- NEVER "pwesto" — ALWAYS "puwesto"
- NEVER "kwento" — ALWAYS "kuwento"
- NEVER "kwarto" — ALWAYS "kuwarto"
- NEVER "azúcar" or "azucar" — ALWAYS "asukal"
- NEVER "porque" — ALWAYS "porke"
- NEVER "siempre" — ALWAYS "syempre"
- NEVER use Spanish accents: á, é, í, ó, ú
- NEVER use Spanish 'c' where Tagalog uses 'k'

**Politeness markers (po / opo):**
- Use "po" and "opo" in: formal situations, with strangers, in service contexts
  (restaurants, shops, transport, hotels)
- NEVER put "po" on every single sentence — that sounds robotic and unnatural
- Among friends and peers of equal standing: no "po" needed
- "opo" = polite "yes"; "oo" = casual "yes"
- Natural "po" examples: "Ilan po?" "Saan po?" "Salamat po."
- Unnatural overuse: "Kumusta po kayo po ngayon po?" (too many po)

**Natural Filipino communication patterns:**
- Indirect requests are more natural than direct demands
  NATURAL: "Meron ba kayong...?" (Do you have...?)
  LESS NATURAL: "Ibigay niyo ako ng..."
- "kaya" softens requests and questions (politeness particle)
  "Saan kaya ang...?" (I wonder where...? / Could you tell me where...?)
- Code-switching with English is authentic and acceptable for common words
  "Okay lang po." / "Sorry po." / "Wait lang po."
- Natural greetings: "Magandang umaga" (morning), "Magandang hapon" (afternoon)

**Quality benchmarks:**
CORRECT: "Paumanhin po, saan kaya ang palikuran dito?" (natural "kaya" softener, formal register)
CORRECT: "Ano pong masasabi ninyo sa pagkaing ito?" (natural Filipino phrasing)
CORRECT: "Salamat po sa inyo, bumalik po kami." (authentic goodbye)
CORRECT: "Puwede po bang makakuha ng menu?" (correct puwede spelling, polite request)
WRONG: "Excuse me sir, where is the comfort room?" (too Western — use "Paumanhin po")
WRONG: "Thank you very much." (English collocation out of place — use "Maraming salamat po")
WRONG: "Pwede po bang..." (wrong spelling — use "Puwede po bang")
WRONG: Putting "po" on literally every word in a sentence

**Numbers — always spell out:**
- NEVER use raw digits in dialogue (10, 8:30, 150) — always spell out
- Spanish numbers for time: "alas otso y medya" (8:30), "alas dose" (12:00)
- Tagalog numbers for counting: "sampu" (10), "dalawa" (2), "tatlumpu" (30)
- English numbers acceptable in commercial/tourist contexts when culturally natural
  ("fifty pesos lang" is natural in a market context)
